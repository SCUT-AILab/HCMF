Title Late Fusion via Subspace Search with Consistency Preservation
Masked Reviewer ID: Assigned_Reviewer_1

Question 
Paper Summary. Please summarize in your own words what the paper is about. This paper studies the problem of late-fusion of features obtained from different modalities/views. Main contributions include (i) a matrix factorization approach to fuse features from different domains, it places a hard constraint on rank to maximize consistency; (ii) an ALM-based algorithm to with with the non-smooth loss, e.g., L_12. The fusion strategy is evaluated on a few commonly used datasets.
Paper Strengths. Positive aspects of the paper. Be sure to comment on the paper's novelty, technical correctness, clarity and experimental evaluation. Notice that different papers may need different levels of evaluation: e.g., a theoretical paper vs. an application paper Pros:
- technical expositions are on the rigorous side, especially the theoretical convergence analysis
- the paper is relatively easy to follow
Paper Weaknesses. Discuss the negative aspects: lack of novelty or clarity, technical errors, insufficient experimental evaluation, etc. Please justify your comments in great detail. If you think the paper is not novel, explain why and provide evidences Cons:
- not entirely convinced by the narrow focus on late fusion; I certainly buy the larger angle of better view/modality fusion, but narrowing the investigation towards late fusion only, while neglecting other means of closing the domain gap is rather harsh; I would be convinced if experiments are set up in a way that they not only compares the proposed with late fusion methods, but other paradigms, e.g., the many weight sharing strategies where fusion is implicitly tackled at training
- experiments not super convincing, (i) RCEC seems to be doing rather well, while being a lot faster on average, (ii) I fail to see why doing (a bit) better than ResNeXt by fusing inferior models somehow justifies the benefits of the proposed method.
Preliminary Rating Weak Reject
Preliminary Evaluation. Please indicate to the AC, your fellow reviewers, and the authors your current opinion on the paper. Please summarize the key things you would like the authors to include in their rebuttals to facilitate your decision making. Overall, this paper has its merits on the technical side, though I am largely let down by its narrow focus, and lack of comparison with the larger field of feature fusion -- the Joint Bayesian work might be a good starting point, see "D. Chen, X. Cao, L. Wang, F. Wen, and J. Sun. Bayesian face revisited: A joint formulation. In ECCV, 2012".

Paper Summary. Please summarize in your own words what the paper is about. This paper proposes a late fusion method, which can combine the predictions from multiple features/classifiers. The proposed approach formulates the late fusion task as a matrix recovery problem, and uses matrix low-rank constraint to achieve consistency in the results. Experiments are run on a couple of public datasets.
Paper Strengths. Positive aspects of the paper. Be sure to comment on the paper's novelty, technical correctness, clarity and experimental evaluation. Notice that different papers may need different levels of evaluation: e.g., a theoretical paper vs. an application paper The paper is generally clearly written. It proposes a new way to do late fusion from multiple predications. The proposed approach formulates the late fusion task as a matrix recovery problem, and uses matrix low-rank constraint to achieve consistency in the results.
Multiple deep neural networks are used as a in experiments.
Paper Weaknesses. Discuss the negative aspects: lack of novelty or clarity, technical errors, insufficient experimental evaluation, etc. Please justify your comments in great detail. If you think the paper is not novel, explain why and provide evidences Features from multiple deep neural networks are used in experiments, and predications are learned via SVM from these features. The predications are then used as input for the proposed late fusion method. To make the work more convincing, it would be desirable to see how the SVM-predication late fusion results compared with the predictions learn end-to-end via a neural network. It would be interesting to see how it compared with neural network based fusion method. Also, it is unclear how the "n" (the number of instances) would impact the results.
Preliminary Rating Weak Reject
